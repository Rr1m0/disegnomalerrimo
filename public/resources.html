Tutorials

I find having too many options confusing, so I follow only a small amount of websites:

➡ The Virtual Instructor | site | youtube |

This is the only drawing course I'm subscribed to. I think Matt Fussell is a great teacher and I like his teaching style a lot. (I love his accent, too!)
His course about drawing fundaments really opened my eyes and he's one of the reasons why I fell in love with observational drawing.

There are a lot of useful free tutorials on his site and youtube channel, but if you subscribe to the site you have access to weekly videos and full courses.
My favourite feature of the subscription is the weekly "Member's Minute", where he critique drawings people send him.

He also runs sketchbooknation, a site with simple, easy to follow guided sketches. You can register to the mailing list (for free + no spam!) and receive a different prompt every day for 30 days.

★ You CAN Draw - How to Draw Webinar
★ The #1 Secret to Drawing

➡ Love Life Drawing | youtube |

A channel specialized in Life Gesture Figure drawing, by Kenzo and his mother Mayko.
Kenzo started learning to draw somewhat later in life and shares videos about struggles he and other people had with getting confidence to draw as older artists.
Relatable and motivating.

★ The 5 stages of learning to draw people
★ Is it TOO LATE to learn to draw & be a good artist?

➡ SarahBurnsStudio | site | youtube |

Incredible Plein Air Artist located in Scotland. She mainly paints landscapes in gouache and watercolour. I really like her painting style and the vulnerability she shows in some of her videos.
She curates a blog, fearlessbrush.com, where you can also find this insanely useful Gouache Database.

➡ Kristin Van Leuven | youtube |

Beginner friendly Watercolour tutorials. Mainly small paintings (flowers) and decorative patterns for cards.

➡ The Etherington Brothers | site |

Authors of How to THINK when you DRAW. Easy to follow drawing and design tutorials. Updated almost daily!
List of the first 300 tutorials in alphabetical order.

Books

➡ Nature Drawing and Journaling by John Muir Laws

A book aimed at people who are into drawing from life and want to keep a "nature" journal.

I myself don't do nature journaling, but I have my own daily journaling habit (I simply draw whatever is on my mind or what is right in front of me) and this book is an exceptionally valuable resource for me. His kind pedagogical approach and the very clear concise instructions are incredibly motivating.

He covers a lot of different traditional media: pencil, ink, watercolour, watercolour pencils and a tiny bit of gouache.
Basically everything I like to use on a daily basis. There is a starting pack "how to" for all these artistic media and a lot of useful tips for beginners who are just approaching drawing as a whole.

Topics covered: how to keep a scientific nature journal; basic drawing fundamentals; how to draw flora (flowers, trees), fungi, fauna (birds seem to be his passion), rocks, landscapes.

Not recommended if you want to learn human anatomy.

※John Muir Laws has a website where you can browse through different resources and even book workshops. You can buy this book through his website.

➡ Huge List of Art Books Reviews by Parka blogs

A good starting point when you are interested in buying an art book, but have no idea if the content would interest you. Every review has photos.
Beware that he takes a commission with some affiliated links, so some book review could be biased. (I personally found no proof of that)
The affiliation is properly advertised under each review.

➡ Book Reviews & Recommendations by Marshall Vandruff

Succinct list of recommended books for beginners. Mostly on Human and Animal Anatomy (it lacks books on landscapes and nature drawings).
