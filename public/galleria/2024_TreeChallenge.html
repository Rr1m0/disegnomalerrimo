Guidelines:

  1. Posting can be delayed (bi-weekly or by the end of the month depending on my personal schedule), but the drawing should be completed by the end of the relative week.
  2. I can complete the challenge in any order I want, but I have to draw ALL the tree types by the end of the challenge.
  3. Only ONE complete drawing/study a week. Spacing them is important to see any progress.
  4. The drawings should be studies and as complete as possible with my current skill set.
  5. No simple rough sketches allowed (or better, allowed as long as it's complementary to a more complete drawing).
  6. All medium allowed. Graphite, Watercolour, Gouache, Ink... Everything's OK.
  7. Notes in any language allowed, as long as it's comprehensible to me.
  8. Have fun!

Why make it a challenge?

Trees and flora in general are the most difficult subjects to draw for me personally.
Problem is: I love the thought of drawing trees and one day becoming able to draw beautiful landscapes full of trees and bushes, but I'm overwelmed by the amount of stuff I need to learn.
Making it a challenge with myself gives me a loose guideline to follow and a set schedule to pace myself and try to give my best even if my drawing skills are poor.

Why make it public?

Because why not? Making it public should be an added incentive to make the studies as thoughtful as possible and make me accountable.
Also, I can come back to them more easily if the studies are available on my website and record my progress (hopefully). It's all very utilitaristic.

If the challenge is successful and provide value to me and my learning process I will do other challenges throught 2024, possibly about Flowers and Architecture, but we will see how my artistic goals change!
